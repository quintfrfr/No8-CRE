Part (a) & (b) Balances & Equations

Mole balance for a packed bed reactor (PBR):

$$\frac{dX}{dW} = \frac{-r'_A}{F_{A0}}$$

Rate law for this elementary reversible reaction \(A + B \rightleftharpoons 2C\):

$$-r'_A = k(T)\left[C_A \cdot C_B - \frac{C_C^2}{K_C(T)}\right]$$

From \(A + B \rightleftharpoons 2C\): stoichiometric coefficients \(a=1,\; b=1,\; c=2\)

Expansion factor check — since \(\delta = 2-(1+1)=0\), then \(\epsilon = y_{A0}\cdot\delta = 0\). Thus volumetric flow rate is solely a function of \(T\) and \(P\).

$$\Theta_A = \frac{F_{A0}}{F_{A0}} = 1, \quad \Theta_B = \frac{F_{B0}}{F_{A0}} = \frac{2F_{A0}}{F_{A0}} = 2, \quad \Theta_C = 0, \quad \Theta_I = \frac{10}{5} = 2$$ $$\nu_A = \nu_B = -1, \quad \nu_C = +2$$

Concentrations as functions of \(X\), \(p = P/P_0\), and \(T\) (with \(\epsilon = 0\)):

$$C_A = C_{A0}(1-X)\,p\!\left(\frac{T_0}{T}\right)$$ $$C_B = C_{A0}(2-X)\,p\!\left(\frac{T_0}{T}\right)$$ $$C_C = C_{A0}(2X)\,p\!\left(\frac{T_0}{T}\right)$$

Substituting all concentrations into the rate law:

$$-r'_A = k(T)\,C_{A0}^2\left[(1-X)(2-X) - \frac{4X^2}{K_C(T)}\right]p^2\!\left(\frac{T_0}{T}\right)^{\!2}$$

Temperature-dependent rate constants via Arrhenius and van't Hoff:

$$k(T) = 0.0002\exp\!\left[\frac{25{,}000}{1.987}\!\left(\frac{1}{300}-\frac{1}{T}\right)\right]$$ $$K_C(T) = 1000\exp\!\left[\frac{-20{,}000}{1.987}\!\left(\frac{1}{305}-\frac{1}{T}\right)\right]$$
Part (c) Equilibrium Conversion \(X_e\) vs Temperature

At equilibrium, \(-r'_A \to 0\), so the bracketed term equals zero:

$$\left(1-X_e\right)\!\left(2-X_e\right) - \frac{4X_e^2}{K_C(T)} = 0$$

Expanding and rearranging, then multiplying through by \(\dfrac{K_C}{4}\):

$$2 - 3X_e + X_e^2 - \frac{4X_e^2}{K_C} = 0 \qquad \times\frac{K_C}{4}$$ $$\left(\frac{K_C}{4}-1\right)X_e^2 - \frac{3K_C}{4}\,X_e + \frac{K_C}{2} = 0$$

Applying the quadratic formula, with \(a = \dfrac{K_C}{4}-1\), \(b = -\dfrac{3K_C}{4}\), \(c = \dfrac{K_C}{2}\):

$$X_e = \frac{\dfrac{3K_C}{4} - \sqrt{\left(\dfrac{3K_C}{4}\right)^{\!2} - 2K_C\!\left(\dfrac{K_C}{4}-1\right)}}{2\!\left(\dfrac{K_C}{4}-1\right)}$$

Part (e) Adiabatic Energy Balance

General energy balance (non-adiabatic):

$$T = T_0 + \frac{X\!\left[-\Delta H_{Rx}(T_r) + \Delta C_p(T_r - T_0)\right]}{\displaystyle\sum\Theta_i C_{p_i} + X\,\Delta C_p}$$

Since \(\Delta C_p = 0\), the adiabatic energy balance simplifies to:

$$T = T_0 + \frac{X\,(-\Delta H_{Rx}^\circ)}{\displaystyle\sum\Theta_i C_{p_i}}$$

Substituting values \(\bigl(T_0=325\;\text{K},\;{-\Delta H_{Rx}^\circ}=20{,}000\;\text{cal/mol},\;\sum\Theta_i C_{p_i}=96\;\text{cal/mol·K}\bigr)\):

$$T = 325 + \frac{20{,}000}{96}\,X \quad \Rightarrow \quad T = 325 + 208.33\,X$$
Part (f) Plot \(X_e,\,X,\,p,\,T\) vs Catalyst Weight \(W\)

For a packed bed with pressure drop (Ergun equation, dimensionless form). Since \(\epsilon = 0\):

$$\frac{dp}{dW} = -\frac{\alpha}{2p}\!\left(\frac{T}{T_0}\right), \qquad \text{where }\alpha = 0.00015\;\text{kg}^{-1}$$

Full ODE system solved simultaneously:

Analysis:
Pressure profile: Because \(T\) is rising (gas expands and flows faster), the pressure drop actually accelerates slightly as the bed moves down.
Temperature profile: Starts rising rapidly above \(T_0\) since the reaction is fast at the entrance. As the reaction slows down it will flatten out (stagnant).
Conversion (X and \(X_e\)): Actual \(X\) starts at 0 and rises upward. At the same time, because temp. is rising, the \(X_e\) starts high and drops downwards. The intersected line is the absolute max. \(X\) the adiabatic reactor could achieve.

Part (g) Isothermal Operation — Heat Removal Profile

To maintain isothermal operation, the heat removed by cooling jacket must perfectly match the heat generated by the reaction at every single point down the reactor.

$$\text{Local Heat Generated} = (-r'_A)(-\Delta H^\circ_{Rx})$$

To find the cumulative heat removed \(\dot{Q}\) as the reactor moves down, set a new differential equation to add up that heat along the catalyst weight \(W\):

$$\frac{d\dot{Q}}{dW} = (-r'_A)(-\Delta H^\circ_{Rx}) = (-r'_A)\left(20{,}000\;\frac{\text{cal}}{\text{mol}}\right)$$

To find volume \(V\), where \(W = \rho_b\,V\) with \(\rho_b = 1.4\;\text{kg/dm}^3\):

$$V = \frac{W}{\rho_b} = \frac{W}{1.4}\;\left[\text{dm}^3\right]$$
